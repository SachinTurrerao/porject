Math Problems and Solutions

1. Crossing Time of Two Trains from Station X and Y

Train A leaves Station X at 5 AM and reaches Station Y at 9 AM. Train B leaves Station Y at 7 AM and reaches Station X at 10:30 AM. To find the time they cross, we calculate their relative speeds and the distance between the stations. The trains will cross each other at around 7:56 AM.

2. Krishna's Total Distance Covered by Train and Foot

Krishna travels part of the journey by train at 25 km/h and the rest on foot at 4 km/h. Total time for the journey is 5 hours and 48 minutes. The total distance covered is 40 km (20 km by train and 20 km by foot).

3. Time Taken by Two Trains to Cross Each Other

The speeds of the two trains are in the ratio of 7:9. The first train takes 4 seconds to cross a pole, and the second train takes 6 seconds. The time taken for the trains to cross each other completely is 10 seconds.

4. Catch-Up Time for Two Buses from Delhi to Karnal

The faster bus, starting at 10 AM at 25 m/s, will catch the slower bus, which started at 8 AM at 20 m/s, at 12:40 PM. This is calculated based on the distance between Delhi and Karnal (200 km).

5. Time for a Car to Cover 630 km with Increasing Speed

The car starts at 50 km/h, with its speed increasing by 5 km/h every hour. The car will take 9 hours to cover the total distance of 630 km.

6. Distance Covered by a Bus at Different Speeds

The bus travels at 50 km/h for 12 minutes and 40 km/h for 18 minutes. The total distance covered by the bus during this time is 23 km.
